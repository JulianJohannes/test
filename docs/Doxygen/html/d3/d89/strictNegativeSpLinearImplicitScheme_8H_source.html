26 Class
27  Foam::strictNegativeSpLinearImplicitScheme
28 
29 Description
30  The class strictNegativeSpLinearImplicitScheme implements an implicit discretization of the source term that ensures contributions to the diagonal dominance of the resulting algebraic linear system.
31 
32  The linearization constants are computed as
33  \f{equation}{
34  \begin{aligned}
35  S_{strictNegativeSpLinearImplicit,\,c} &= \max(f_{nl}(\psi^n),0)\psi^n_c\\
36  S_{strictNegativeSpLinearImplicit,\,p} &= \min(f_{nl}(\psi^n),0),
37  \end{aligned}
38  \f}
39  which ensures that the constant \f$S_p \le 0\f$ for all cells \cite patankar_numerical_1980.
40  The resulting source term, written as
41  \f{equation}{
42  S_{strictNegativeSpLinearImplicit}(\psi^{n+1}) = \max(f_{nl}(\psi^n),0)\psi^n_c
43  + \min(f_{nl}(\psi^n),0)\psi^{n+1}_c,
44  \f}
45  thus switches between explicit and implicit discretization cell by cell and depending on the sign of \f$f(\psi^n)\f$.
46  This addresses the problem that unconditional implicit treatment can cause the solution to diverge. The reason
47  is that a positive \f$S_p\f$ reduces the diagonal coefficient in the assembled linear system. This harms the diagonal dominance of the matrix,
48  which may then lose its M-matrix and discrete stability property.
